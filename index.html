Generating tones to realize both pitch circularity and vowel-like perception

Kaori Hashimoto, Takao Kawamura, Nobutaka Ono

Sound Demos

This section presents musical examples composed of the circularly-pitched singing voice. The normal singing voice were synthesized using Sinsy [1], and then pitch-manipulated using WORLD [2]. For more details, please refer to our paper.

Boléro / Maurice Ravel (Composed)

Both normal and circularly-pitched singing voice use 12 semitone-spaced tones within one octave (C3 to B3). Note that the original sound's score of ``Boléro'' spans 14 semitones (C3 to D4), although the interval between adjacent tones is never more than five semitones.
